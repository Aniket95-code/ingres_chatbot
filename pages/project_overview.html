Understanding INGRES

Explore the scientific workflow and groundwater management challenges that drive our AI-powered solution for India's water resource professionals.

What is INGRES?

INGRES (Integrated Groundwater Resource Evaluation System) is India's comprehensive scientific framework for assessing and managing groundwater resources across diverse geological and climatic conditions.

The system integrates hydrogeological surveys, geophysical investigations, and water quality assessments to provide actionable insights for sustainable groundwater management at national, state, and local levels.

  • Hydrogeological Mapping: Detailed aquifer characterization using geological surveys and borehole data analysis
  • Geophysical Investigations: Electrical resistivity and seismic surveys for subsurface water detection
  • Water Quality Assessment: Chemical and biological analysis following BIS standards
  • Data Integration: GIS-based spatial analysis and modeling for resource estimation

Critical Challenges

Over-exploitation Crisis

Over 60% of India's groundwater blocks are experiencing declining water levels due to excessive extraction for agriculture and urban needs.

Data Accessibility Gap

Complex scientific data remains inaccessible to field professionals, limiting effective decision-making at the grassroots level.

Regional Variations

Diverse geological conditions across states require localized assessment approaches and region-specific solutions.
